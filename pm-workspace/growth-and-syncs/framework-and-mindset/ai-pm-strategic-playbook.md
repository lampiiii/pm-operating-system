# AI PM Strategic Playbook

> **Purpose:** A north star template for career positioning in the Agentic AI era. This playbook defines who you need to become, the skills to build, and the actions that will get you to the Strategic Apex.

---

# The Core Belief

The PM role is **not disappearing — it's splitting into two tracks:**

| Track | What It Looks Like | Future |
|---|---|---|
| **Task Execution** | Writing tickets, gathering requirements, coordinating delivery | Replaced by agents |
| **Strategic Apex** | Vision, judgment, governance, stakeholder alignment | Irreplaceable — this is where I want to be |

**The goal: move up to the Strategic Apex before the gap closes.** Customize the sections below with your own OKRs, ownership areas, and manager directives.

# The Future PM Role

A Product Manager must focus on five things:

1. **Setting product vision** — own the "where are we going" question
2. **Navigating ambiguity** — make decisions when data is incomplete
3. **Aligning the organization around strategy** — not just your team, the whole org
4. **Orchestrating AI agent systems** — design, supervise, and govern agents
5. **System-level thinking** — operate across the full system, not just one feature

---

# What You Need to Become

## System-Level Thinker

Think across the whole system — not just one product or feature.

- Own **pricing strategy** in a world where AI compute costs compress margins
- Govern **hybrid human–AI workforces** (humans + agents working together)
- Build deep **domain expertise** that agents can't replicate
- Drive **influence without authority** across orgs
- Master **stakeholder management** at the executive layer

## AI Workflow Designer + Agent Supervisor

Don't just use agents — design them and supervise them.

- Design the **workflows agents will execute**
- Define **decision boundaries** — what agents decide vs. escalate
- Set **guardrails and trust mechanisms** inside agent loops
- **Evaluate and select** agent architectures — commit to a direction (not just generate options)

**Key principle:** Judgment in evaluating, selecting, and committing to a strategic direction becomes the scarce asset — not the generation of options itself.

# Skills That AI Cannot Replace (Your Moat)

| Skill | Why It's Irreplaceable |
|---|---|
| **Judgment** | Choosing *which* direction to go — not just generating options. This becomes the scarce act. |
| **Design Thinking** | Observing what customers actually *do* (not just what they say). Requires empathy + presence. |
| **Read the Room** | Cross-functional leadership, trust-building, sensing org dynamics. Pure human skill. |
| **Ethical AI Governance** | Embedding trust and guardrails into agent decision loops. High-stakes, high-judgment work. |
| **Pricing Strategy** | Accounting for AI compute costs, margin compression, enterprise deal structures. Value-based pricing for AI-powered products. |
| **Domain Expertise** | Deep knowledge of compliance frameworks, industry workflows, enterprise integrations, customer operations. |
| **Proof of Value** | Creating real customer outcomes — not just demos. Deliver measurable outcomes, not capability showcases. |

# Career Positioning: M-Shaped System Orchestrator

The "M-shape" means: two areas of deep expertise, connected by broad systems thinking.

```
        [STRATEGIC APEX]
       /                 \
  Depth 1             Depth 2
  Domain              Agent/AI
  Expertise           Governance
       \                 /
        [PLATFORM THINKING]
        (Systems Orchestration)
```

## Platform Thinking Is the New Technical Bar

> **PMs who understand API strategy, developer ecosystems, and cross-system orchestration hold a structural advantage.**

This doesn't mean coding. It means:
- Understanding how systems talk to each other
- Knowing what's possible at the integration layer
- Designing products that work *across* platforms, not just within one
- Understanding **platform architecture** and developer ecosystems

## What the Strategic Apex Requires

These are the domains you need to own to sit at the top:

- **Business strategy** — connecting product decisions to company outcomes
- **Organisation design** — how teams and agents should be structured
- **AI governance** — trust, guardrails, ethics in agent decision-making
- **Cross-domain AI fluency** — understanding AI across multiple business functions
- **Pricing** — especially AI SKU, compute-aware pricing models
- **Enterprise sales alignment** — knowing how GTM and product intersect
- **Compliance** — especially in regulated industries
- **Change management** — driving adoption of AI-powered workflows
- **Strategic evaluation frameworks** — structured approaches to assess and commit to directions
- **Technical frontier awareness** — staying current on what's possible with AI

# Action Framework: How to Apply This Daily

## Every Decision — Ask Yourself

1. **Am I operating at the Strategic Apex or the Task Execution layer?** If task execution, can I delegate or automate it?
2. **Am I deepening a moat skill?** (Judgment, domain expertise, governance, pricing, design thinking)
3. **Am I creating proof of value?** Can I tie this work to a measurable customer or business outcome?
4. **Am I thinking at the system level?** Or am I stuck in a single feature?

## Weekly Check-In

- Which moat skill did I exercise this week?
- What judgment call did I make (not just options I generated)?
- Where did I influence without authority?
- What outcome can I point to — not just output?
