Strategic Playbook

Career positioning for the Agentic AI era
NORTH STAR
"Be the PM who sits at the Strategic Apex: the one who sets vision, governs AI systems, owns domain depth, and creates proof of value — not the one who writes tickets that agents will replace."
TWO TRACKS
The PM role is splitting into two tracks
Being Replaced Task Execution
Looks like: Writing tickets, gathering requirements, coordinating delivery
Future: Replaced by agents
Irreplaceable Strategic Apex
Looks like: Vision, judgment, governance, stakeholder alignment
Future: Irreplaceable — this is where I want to be
FUTURE PM ROLE
Five things a PM must focus on
  • 1 Setting product vision — own the "where are we going" question
  • 2 Navigating ambiguity — make decisions when data is incomplete
  • 3 Aligning the organization around strategy — not just your team, the whole org
  • 4 Orchestrating AI agent systems — design, supervise, and govern agents
  • 5 System-level thinking — operate across the full system, not just one feature
WHAT I NEED TO BECOME
Two complementary skill profiles

Profile 1 System-Level Thinker

  • Own pricing strategy in a world where AI compute costs compress margins
  • Govern hybrid human-AI workforces (humans + agents working together)
  • Build deep domain expertise that agents can't replicate
  • Drive influence without authority across orgs
  • Master stakeholder management at the executive layer

Profile 2 AI Workflow Designer + Agent Supervisor

  • Design the workflows agents will execute
  • Define decision boundaries — what agents decide vs. escalate
  • Set guardrails and trust mechanisms inside agent loops
  • Evaluate and select agent architectures — commit to a direction
Key principle: Judgment in evaluating, selecting, and committing to a strategic direction becomes the scarce asset — not the generation of options itself.
MOAT SKILLS
Skills that AI cannot replace
Judgment
Choosing which direction to go — not just generating options. This becomes the scarce act.
Design Thinking
Observing what customers actually do (not just what they say). Requires empathy + presence.
Read the Room
Cross-functional leadership, trust-building, sensing org dynamics. Pure human skill.
Ethical AI Governance
Embedding trust and guardrails into agent decision loops. High-stakes, high-judgment work.
Pricing Strategy
Accounting for AI compute costs, margin compression, enterprise deal structures. Value-based pricing for AI-powered products.
Domain Expertise
Deep knowledge of compliance frameworks, industry workflows, enterprise integrations, customer operations.
Proof of Value
Creating real customer outcomes — not just demos. Deliver measurable outcomes, not capability showcases.

ACTION FRAMEWORK
How to apply this daily

Every Decision — Ask Yourself

  1. Am I operating at the Strategic Apex or the Task Execution layer? If task execution, can I delegate or automate it?
  2. Am I deepening a moat skill? (Judgment, domain expertise, governance, pricing, design thinking)
  3. Am I creating proof of value? Can I tie this work to a measurable customer or business outcome?
  4. Am I thinking at the system level? Or am I stuck in a single feature?

Weekly Check-In

  • Which moat skill did I exercise this week?
  • What judgment call did I make (not just options I generated)?
  • Where did I influence without authority?
  • What outcome can I point to — not just output?
