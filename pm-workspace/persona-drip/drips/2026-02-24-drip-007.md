# 🎯 Today's Persona Drip

**Scenario:** Product ships a breaking change — CSM finds out from the customer

**Time:** Monday, 8:47 AM

**Context:** A CSM opens Slack on Monday morning to 4 messages from a $500K enterprise account's VP of Customer Operations: "Our automations stopped working over the weekend. We have board reporting that depends on this. What happened?"

---

**The Play-by-Play:**

- Reads the Slack messages with growing dread. The customer is not just annoyed — they're panicked. Board reporting runs on Monday morning.
- Searches internal Slack for any deployment notifications. Finds one in #engineering-deploys from Friday at 5:47 PM: "Deployed v3.8.2 — API response format changes for /accounts endpoint. Migration guide in docs."
- Nobody tagged CS. Nobody tagged her. The "migration guide" is a developer doc that no customer would find.
- Opens Jira to check the release notes. The ticket says "breaking change" in small text under the description. No customer communication was planned.
- Messages the engineering team lead: "Hey, the API format change in v3.8.2 broke automations for Apex Corp. They have board reporting that ran this morning. Can we get a rollback or a quick fix?"
- Response: "We can't roll back — other customers are already on v3.8.2. We can provide a migration script. ETA: 4 hours."
- Four hours. The board meeting is in 2 hours.
- Jumps on an emergency call with the customer. Walks them through a manual workaround for the specific report they need today. It takes 45 minutes of screen-sharing.
- After the call, writes an internal post-mortem request: "Breaking API changes need CS notification 48 hours before deploy. This is the third time this quarter a customer found out before we did."
- Updates the account health score manually — this incident will suppress the automated score, but she wants the context captured.
- Spends the rest of the morning checking her other enterprise accounts for the same API dependency. Two more are affected but haven't noticed yet. She reaches out proactively.

---

**The Invisible Friction:**

- There's no system connecting product releases to affected customers. The CSM is the manual integration layer between engineering deploys and customer impact.
- "Breaking change" in a Jira ticket doesn't trigger any CS workflow. The classification exists but nothing acts on it.
- Proactively checking other accounts required the CSM to manually know which customers use which API endpoints. There's no feature-to-account mapping.

---

**PM Takeaway:**

Every breaking change is a trust event. If your CS team finds out about product changes from customers instead of from the product team, you have a communication architecture problem, not a people problem. Build the connective tissue between deploy pipelines and customer impact maps.
