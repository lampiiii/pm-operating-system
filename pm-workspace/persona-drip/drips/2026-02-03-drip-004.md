# 🎯 Today's Persona Drip

**Scenario:** The QBR that sells itself — when prep meets opportunity

**Time:** Wednesday, 9:30 AM

**Context:** A $280K enterprise account has their quarterly business review in 3 hours. The CSM has been prepping since last week, but this morning she noticed something in the data: the customer's secondary team just hit 90% adoption — they weren't even in the original deal. This QBR just went from "status update" to "expansion conversation."

---

**The Play-by-Play:**

- Opens the account dashboard at 9:30. Health score: 82 (up from 64 last quarter). Usage is up 35% across all teams.
- Notices the analytics team — 14 users who weren't in the original contract — has been active for 6 weeks. They're power users already.
- Pulls up the usage breakdown by department. The analytics team alone is generating more API calls than the original sales ops team.
- Realizes she needs to reframe the entire QBR. This isn't a "how are things going" meeting — it's a "you've already expanded, let's formalize it" meeting.
- Opens the QBR deck template. Spends 20 minutes rebuilding slide 3 to show the adoption curve by department, with the analytics team highlighted.
- Crafts a "value realized" slide: hours saved, deals influenced, data accuracy improvement. Uses the customer's own usage data.
- Slack messages the AE: "Hey, I think we have a natural expansion play for the Meridian QBR today. Their analytics team adopted organically — 14 users, heavy usage. Can you join for the last 15 min?"
- AE responds: "Wait, really? I had no idea. Absolutely joining."
- Rehearses her transition from "success review" to "what's next" — she doesn't want it to feel like a bait and switch.
- Joins the Zoom. The VP of Revenue Ops opens with: "We love the tool. In fact, my analytics team has been using it too — can we talk about getting them proper licenses?"
- The CSM smiles. The QBR just sold itself.

---

**The Invisible Friction:**

- The CSM had no automated alert when a new department started using the product. She found the analytics team's usage by accident while prepping the QBR.
- The AE had zero visibility into product usage data. The expansion signal was sitting in the CSM's dashboard but never surfaced to sales.
- Building the "value realized" slide required manually pulling data from three different sources and doing the math in a spreadsheet.

---

**PM Takeaway:**

Expansion revenue hides in usage data that nobody is watching. If your product can detect organic adoption by new teams and automatically notify both CSM and AE, you're turning a lucky discovery into a repeatable revenue motion.
