# 🎯 Today's Persona Drip

**Scenario:** The handoff: transitioning an account from onboarding to long-term CSM

**Time:** Wednesday, 11:00 AM

**Context:** A $120K mid-market account just completed their 45-day onboarding. The onboarding specialist is handing off to the long-term CSM. On paper, everything looks great — all milestones checked. But the incoming CSM has learned to never trust the checklist.

---

**The Play-by-Play:**

- The incoming CSM opens the account in the platform. Onboarding status: "Complete." Health score: 75. All green checkmarks. Looks perfect.
- Reads the onboarding notes. They're sparse: "Customer completed all milestones. Primary contact: Lisa Park, Director of CS Ops. No open issues."
- Digs deeper. Pulls up the actual usage data. The customer activated 3 of 5 modules. The two unused modules — analytics and automation — were the ones highlighted in the sales pitch as key value drivers.
- Checks the engagement data. Lisa logged in 22 times during onboarding. But the other 8 users on the license? Average logins: 1.4. They signed up and never came back.
- Joins the handoff call with the onboarding specialist. Asks: "What happened with the analytics and automation modules?" Answer: "Lisa said they'd get to those in Phase 2. I logged it in the notes."
- The "notes" are a single line buried in a text field. No follow-up task was created. No timeline for Phase 2 was set.
- After the handoff call, the CSM creates her own action plan: intro call with Lisa next week, check on Phase 2 timeline, and run a user adoption workshop for the 8 inactive users.
- Sends Lisa a warm handoff email: personal, not templated. Mentions a specific insight from the usage data — "I noticed your team is getting great value from the health score module — averaging 4 sessions per week. I'd love to help you unlock similar wins with analytics."
- Privately notes her concern: this account has a 75 health score but only 60% feature adoption and 12% user activation beyond the primary contact. The score is lying.
- Adds a tag to the account: "Handoff Risk — Low User Adoption." She'll check back in 2 weeks.

---

**The Invisible Friction:**

- The onboarding checklist measures task completion, not value realization. "Complete" onboarding doesn't mean the customer is set up for success.
- The handoff between onboarding and long-term CS loses context. The onboarding specialist's tribal knowledge doesn't transfer through CRM notes.
- Health score doesn't weight user adoption heavily enough. An account with 1 active user out of 9 shouldn't score 75.

---

**PM Takeaway:**

The onboarding-to-CS handoff is where accounts go to quietly die. If your platform's definition of "onboarding complete" is a checklist and not a value threshold, you're shipping half-activated accounts to CSMs and calling it success. Measure activation, not completion.
