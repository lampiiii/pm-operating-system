# 🎯 Today's Persona Drip

**Scenario:** The Friday-afternoon win — the account that came back from red

**Time:** Friday, 5:20 PM

**Context:** Six months ago, a $310K enterprise account was 72 hours from sending a non-renewal email. The CSM ran a save play, rebuilt trust, and today — Friday afternoon — the customer signed a 3-year renewal with a $140K expansion. This drip isn't about the save. It's about what the CSM feels after the save.

---

**The Play-by-Play:**

- Gets the DocuSign notification at 5:18 PM. Signed by both the customer CFO and her own VP. Three-year deal. $450K total.
- Stares at the screen. Doesn't immediately feel what she thought she'd feel. It's quieter than expected.
- Scrolls back through the account timeline. Remembers the October escalation — the angry email from the customer VP of Engineering, the emergency call, the 11 PM Slack messages with her own engineering team.
- Remembers the moment the save started working. Week three of the recovery plan. The customer admin sent a Slack message: "This is the most responsive our vendor has been in a year." She'd cried at her desk.
- Thinks about what almost lost the account. It wasn't the product gap — it was the three weeks of silence after the first complaint. Nobody owned the escalation. She'd inherited it cold.
- Pulls up the champion tracker she built for this account. 14 stakeholders mapped, weekly touchpoints logged, decision-maker sentiment tracked monthly. It's over-engineered for a normal account. It saved this one.
- Opens a new email to her VP. Subject: "Stonebridge renewed — 3yr + $140K expansion." Writes three sentences: what closed, who made it happen, and what she'll replicate. No bragging. Just the facts.
- Adds a line at the bottom: "Would love to turn the playbook we ran here into a template for the team. It's not magic — it's just volume of small touches done consistently."
- Sends it. Leans back. Looks at the clock. 5:34 PM.
- Texts her partner: "Big renewal closed. Leaving now. Let's get pizza." Shuts the laptop.
- On the walk out, passes the CS team's "wall of wins" — a small printed poster in the kitchen with recent renewals. She thinks: I'll put Stonebridge up there Monday. Not tonight. Tonight is just for her.
- Wonders, on the elevator, whether any of this is replicable or whether it was just her, this account, this moment. Decides that's a Monday question.

---

**The Invisible Friction:**

- Saved accounts don't generate institutional learning. The playbook she ran lives in her head — the next CSM facing a similar escalation will reinvent it.
- Recognition systems reward new ARR loudly and saves quietly. A $140K expansion gets a Slack shoutout; the $310K save that enabled it gets a line in a renewal dashboard.
- The platform measures the moment of renewal but not the 90-day recovery trajectory. The data needed to build "save playbooks from successful saves" exists but is never extracted.

---

**PM Takeaway:**

The emotional architecture of CS is asymmetric: losses feel bigger than wins, and wins fade faster than losses. If your platform can capture and replay successful saves as institutional memory — not just dashboards — you're building something that makes CSMs feel less alone. That matters more than any feature list you'll ever ship.
