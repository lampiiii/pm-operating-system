# 🎯 Today's Persona Drip

**Scenario:** The emotional weight of a churn you couldn't prevent

**Time:** Friday, 4:30 PM

**Context:** The CSM just got off a call where a $90K mid-market account told her they're not renewing. She did everything right — proactive outreach, QBRs, escalations — but the customer's company pivoted strategy and the product no longer fits. Sometimes you lose even when you play perfectly.

---

**The Play-by-Play:**

- Hangs up the Zoom call. Sits quietly for a moment. She's been managing this account for 14 months.
- The customer was genuinely apologetic: "It's not your product — it's us. We're moving to an in-house solution because our new CTO wants to build everything internally."
- Opens the CRM and changes the renewal forecast to "Churned — Strategic Pivot." Pauses before hitting save. This number will show up in the weekly report. Her manager will ask about it on Monday.
- Writes detailed churn notes: the timeline, the signals she caught, the interventions she tried, and why none of them could have changed the outcome. This isn't for anyone specific — it's for the next CSM who inherits a similar situation.
- Checks her portfolio. She started the quarter with $2.1M in renewals. She's now at $1.85M after two churns. The target is 92% retention. She's at 88%.
- Thinks about the other churn — that one stung more because she missed the signals. This one is different. She did everything right. But the math doesn't care about context.
- Opens Slack to her CS team channel. Types: "Lost Dataflow today. Strategic pivot, nothing we could've done differently. Notes in the CRM if anyone wants to review the playbook for future reference." Deletes it. Types it again. Posts it.
- Three colleagues react with 💛. One DMs her: "I lost one like that last quarter. Buy you a coffee?"
- Closes her laptop at 5:15 PM. Earlier than usual. Decides to go for a walk before the weekend.
- On Monday, she'll pull up the churn analysis report and add this to the "uncontrollable churn" category. She'll advocate in the product review for better signals around strategic shifts — executive sentiment changes, new leadership hires, budget reallocation patterns.

---

**The Invisible Friction:**

- The retention metric doesn't distinguish between preventable and unpreventable churn. A strategic pivot churn counts the same as a missed-signal churn in the CSM's scorecard.
- There's no system to detect early signals of strategic shifts — new CTO hires, budget freezes, or internal build-vs-buy decisions. These signals exist in public data but aren't surfaced.
- Writing churn notes is manual and unstructured. There's no post-mortem template that captures the "tried and failed" interventions for institutional learning.

---

**PM Takeaway:**

Not all churn is failure. But if your platform can't distinguish between preventable and unpreventable churn, you're burning out your best CSMs by holding them accountable for outcomes they can't control. Build the taxonomy of churn types, and let the data tell the real story.
