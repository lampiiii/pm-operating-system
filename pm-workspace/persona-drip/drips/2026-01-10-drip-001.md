# 🎯 Today's Persona Drip

**Scenario:** Renewal risk — the save playbook kicks in

**Time:** Monday, 2:15 PM

**Context:** A $420K annual account just dropped from green to red on the health scorecard. The renewal is in 47 days. The CSM's manager pinged on Slack: "What's the plan?"

---

**The Play-by-Play:**

- Opens Gainsight, navigates to the account scorecard. Engagement score: 31 (was 68 two weeks ago). Product usage dropped 40% since their internal reorg.
- Checks Salesforce for the opportunity record. Renewal amount: $420K. No expansion opportunity logged. Last executive touchpoint: 3 months ago.
- Scrolls through Timeline in Gainsight — the last meaningful activity was a support escalation about broken SSO integration. Still open. 12 days old.
- Opens Slack, DMs the support engineer: "Hey, what's the latest on the Acme SSO ticket? Their renewal is in 47 days and the health score just tanked."
- Gets a reply: "Engineering deprioritized it. It's in the backlog."
- Feels a knot in her stomach. This is the kind of thing that turns a renewal into a churn.
- Opens a Google Doc and starts drafting a save plan: executive sponsor re-engagement, SSO escalation to engineering leadership, QBR fast-tracked to next week, success plan refresh.
- Sends Slack message to the AE: "Heads up — Acme renewal is at risk. Can we get 30 min tomorrow to align on a save strategy?"
- Updates the health score notes in Gainsight with a manual override explanation.
- Blocks 2 hours tomorrow for the QBR prep she wasn't planning to do this week.

---

**The Invisible Friction:**

- The CSM had no proactive alert when usage started declining — she only found out when the health score algorithm caught up, 2 weeks late.
- The support ticket → renewal risk connection is invisible in the tooling. She had to manually connect those dots.
- Drafting the save plan in Google Docs means it lives outside the CS platform — no tracking, no template, no workflow.

---

**PM Takeaway:**

If your CS platform can't surface the connection between an open support escalation and an upcoming renewal, you're asking CSMs to be the integration layer. That's a product gap, not a process problem.
