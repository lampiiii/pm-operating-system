# 🎯 Today's Persona Drip

**Scenario:** Stale data breaking health scores before a big QBR

**Time:** Tuesday, 9:15 AM

**Context:** The CSM has a $1.4M QBR on Thursday. She's pulling together the deck and notices the adoption metrics look wrong — engagement shows flat when she knows the customer launched a major rollout last month.

---

**The Play-by-Play:**

- Opens the health dashboard. Adoption score: 42. That can't be right — the customer added 300 users last month.
- Clicks into the usage data. Last sync: 18 days ago. The Snowflake data pipeline hasn't run in almost three weeks.
- Slack messages the data engineering team: "Our usage data for GlobalTech hasn't synced since Jan 2. I have a QBR Thursday. Can we get a manual refresh?"
- Gets back: "We're migrating the pipeline. Should be back online next week."
- Stares at the screen. Next week doesn't help with Thursday's QBR.
- Opens Mixpanel directly — the product analytics tool that feeds the CS platform. Manually pulls the login counts, feature adoption rates, and session duration for GlobalTech.
- Copies numbers into a spreadsheet. Manually calculates what the health score *should* be.
- Realizes the real adoption score is 71, not 42. A 29-point gap because of stale data.
- Spends 45 minutes rebuilding the QBR deck with manually corrected numbers, adding footnotes explaining the data lag.
- Makes a mental note to check data freshness before every QBR from now on. Another manual step to remember.

---

**The Invisible Friction:**

- There's no staleness indicator on the health score. The number looks authoritative whether it's 2 hours old or 18 days old.
- The CSM has no way to trigger a manual data refresh — she's dependent on another team's timeline.
- The workaround (manually pulling from Mixpanel) requires access and knowledge that not every CSM has. This is tribal knowledge, not a supported workflow.

---

**PM Takeaway:**

A health score without a freshness signal is worse than no health score — it gives false confidence. If your platform shows stale data as current truth, CSMs will stop trusting the numbers and build shadow spreadsheets.
