# 🎯 Today's Persona Drip

**Scenario:** The Tuesday-morning pipeline — reading 40 accounts before standup

**Time:** Thursday, 8:15 AM

**Context:** A commercial CSM manages 40 accounts in the $25K–$100K band. Before the 9:00 AM team standup, she needs to scan her book, flag anything that shifted overnight, and walk in with three things to say. No time to read every dashboard.

---

**The Play-by-Play:**

- Opens her laptop at 8:15. Coffee in hand. Standup at 9:00.
- First stop: the portfolio view. Sorts by "health score delta — last 7 days." Three accounts dropped. Two are noise (a single low NPS response). One is real — usage down 40% at Northwind.
- Clicks into Northwind. Last touchpoint: her QBR 19 days ago. No support tickets. No product incidents. Just… quiet. The kind of quiet that precedes a non-renewal email.
- Pulls up the usage breakdown. The admin — her champion — hasn't logged in for 12 days. Out sick? On vacation? Job-hunting? Can't tell from here.
- Checks LinkedIn in a second tab. Champion updated her headline 4 days ago: "Open to new opportunities." Heart sinks a little.
- Adds Northwind to her "active concerns" list. Drafts a casual check-in email to the secondary contact — not the champion — asking how Q2 is going. Schedules to send at 10:30 AM (her manager's rule: no "we noticed you've been quiet" emails before 10 AM).
- Scans the next 20 accounts quickly. Looking for three things only: red/yellow flips, overdue renewals inside 60 days, and open support tickets older than 5 days.
- One renewal at $42K is 47 days out. Status: "On Track." Health: green. But the last CSM touchpoint was her onboarding call 11 months ago. She's been on autopilot. Flags it — needs a pre-renewal conversation this week.
- Eight minutes to standup. Opens her notes doc. Types three bullets: Northwind (champion risk), Riverbed (autopilot renewal), Forge Labs (overdue support escalation she promised to drive yesterday).
- Walks into the standup room at 9:01. Reads her three bullets. Sits down. The process works — but only because she built the muscle to read 40 accounts in 40 minutes.

---

**The Invisible Friction:**

- The "health score delta" sort is the single most useful view she has, but it's buried three clicks deep — most CSMs don't find it until month 6.
- Champion-risk signals are scattered across 4 tools (product usage, CRM, LinkedIn, support). There's no single "is this champion at risk?" surface.
- Autopilot renewals — green health, high ARR, zero touchpoints — are invisible until they churn. The system rewards what it measures; it doesn't measure silence.

---

**PM Takeaway:**

The most valuable CSM behavior in the commercial segment isn't deep intervention — it's triage speed. If your platform takes more than 10 minutes to tell a CSM what changed overnight across their book, you're losing them to spreadsheets. Design for the 40-minutes-before-standup use case.
