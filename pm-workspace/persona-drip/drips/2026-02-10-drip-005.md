# 🎯 Today's Persona Drip

**Scenario:** Champion leaves — scrambling to find the new power user

**Time:** Tuesday, 10:45 AM

**Context:** The CSM just got a LinkedIn notification: her primary champion at a $150K mid-market account changed their job title to "VP of Operations at [Different Company]." The renewal is in 60 days. Nobody else at the account has replied to an email in 3 weeks.

---

**The Play-by-Play:**

- Stares at the LinkedIn notification for a full 10 seconds. Sarah was the champion. Sarah was the reason this account renewed last year. Sarah is gone.
- Opens the CRM. Last activity from the account: Sarah's email 3 weeks ago saying "Let's sync after I'm back from PTO." She wasn't on PTO. She was leaving.
- Checks the account's user activity in the product. Sarah's login: 0 sessions in 14 days. But there's one user — "James Rivera, Senior Analyst" — who logged in 11 times last week.
- Searches Slack for any mentions of the account. Nothing from James. The support team had a ticket from him last month about a dashboard filter. It was resolved in 2 hours.
- Drafts a careful email to James: "Hi James, I noticed you've been getting great use out of the dashboards. I'd love to connect and make sure you're getting the most out of the platform. Also happy to share some tips from other teams using similar workflows."
- Does NOT mention Sarah leaving. Does NOT mention the renewal. This has to feel like value, not desperation.
- Updates the account notes: "Champion departed. James Rivera identified as potential new champion based on usage. Renewal at risk — 60 days out. Escalating to manager."
- Sends a Slack message to her manager: "Heads up — lost our champion at Meridian Analytics. Renewal is April 10. I've identified a power user and reaching out today. May need exec-to-exec engagement if the new contact isn't decision-level."
- Blocks 30 minutes tomorrow to research James on LinkedIn and figure out his reporting chain.
- Adds a CTA to her internal save plan: "Get meeting with James by Friday. Identify new economic buyer by end of next week."

---

**The Invisible Friction:**

- The CSM had no alert when her champion's product usage dropped to zero. She found out through LinkedIn, not through her CS platform.
- There's no "champion risk" signal in the health score. A key contact going dark should be a leading indicator, but it isn't modeled.
- Identifying the next power user required manually checking individual login data. The platform doesn't surface "rising users" or "emerging champions."

---

**PM Takeaway:**

Champion departure is the single highest-risk event in a customer relationship, and most CS platforms treat it as invisible. If you can detect when a primary contact goes dark and simultaneously surface who's rising in usage, you turn a crisis into a playbook.
