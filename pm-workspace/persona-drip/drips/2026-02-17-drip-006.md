# 🎯 Today's Persona Drip

**Scenario:** The onboarding call that reveals the real use case

**Time:** Thursday, 2:00 PM

**Context:** Day 5 of onboarding for a new $35K SMB account. The CSM has been walking through the standard onboarding checklist: import contacts, set up health scores, configure alerts. But on today's call, the customer says something that changes everything: "Actually, we don't really care about health scores. We bought this to stop our CEO from calling random customers."

---

**The Play-by-Play:**

- Joins the Zoom with the onboarding deck open. Slide 4: "Configure your health score inputs." She's done this 50 times.
- The customer — a Head of CS at a 40-person startup — interrupts: "Can we skip the health score stuff? Our CEO calls customers directly when he sees a support ticket. It's chaos. We need a single place where he can see everything so he stops freelancing."
- The CSM pauses. This isn't the use case in the sales notes. The sales notes say "health score and playbook automation."
- Quickly pivots: "Got it. Let me show you something different." Pulls up the account overview page and the activity timeline.
- Walks through how the CEO could get a real-time view of every account without needing to call anyone. "He can see the latest support ticket, usage trend, and CSM notes all in one place."
- The customer lights up: "Yes. Exactly. If he can see this dashboard, he'll stop cold-calling our customers at 7 AM."
- Reworks the remaining onboarding plan on the fly. Drops the playbook automation module (week 2) and replaces it with "executive dashboard setup and sharing permissions."
- After the call, updates the CRM with the real use case. Tags it: "executive visibility" — not "health score automation."
- Sends a note to the sales team: "FYI — the actual buyer need was executive visibility, not what's in the opp notes. Might be worth capturing this in discovery next time."
- Adjusts the success criteria for this account: activation = CEO logs in weekly. Not "3 playbooks configured."

---

**The Invisible Friction:**

- The sales-to-CS handoff had the wrong use case. The CSM wasted 4 days onboarding against the wrong goals.
- There's no field in the CRM for "actual use case vs. sold use case." This disconnect happens constantly but is never tracked.
- The standard onboarding checklist is rigid — there's no way to dynamically adjust it based on the customer's real need without manually rebuilding the plan.

---

**PM Takeaway:**

The use case the customer bought isn't always the use case they need. If your onboarding flow can't adapt mid-stream based on real-time discovery, you're optimizing for checklist completion instead of time-to-value. Build for flexibility, not just completeness.
