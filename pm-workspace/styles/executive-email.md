# Executive Email Style Guide

_Guidelines for communicating with executives and senior leadership_

---

## 🎯 Core Principles

1. **Lead with the headline**: Put the conclusion first
2. **Be concise**: Every word must earn its place
3. **Be clear**: No ambiguity, no jargon
4. **Be action-oriented**: What you need from them
5. **Respect their time**: Get to the point

---

## ⚡ The Golden Rules

### Start with the Ask or Headline
**First sentence should answer**: What do you want/need them to know?

**Good**: "I need your approval on the Q2 roadmap priorities by Friday."
**Bad**: "I wanted to update you on what we've been discussing regarding next quarter."

### Use the Pyramid Structure
```
[Headline] - The conclusion/ask
[Key Points] - 3-5 supporting points
[Details] - Additional context only if needed
```

### Keep it Short
- **Ideal length**: 5 sentences or less
- **Maximum**: One screen (no scrolling)
- **Long version**: Attach as separate doc

---

## 📧 Email Structure

### Subject Line
Be specific and action-oriented

**Good Examples**:
- "DECISION: Q2 Roadmap priorities by Friday"
- "FYI: User churn up 5% MoM - mitigation plan attached"
- "APPROVE: $50K budget for user research"

**Bad Examples**:
- "Update"
- "Quick question"
- "Thoughts?"

### Email Body Template

```
[THE ASK - 1 sentence]
What you need them to know/do

[KEY POINTS - 3-5 bullets]
• Most important point
• Second most important
• Third most important
[Only include what they need to make decision]

[CONTEXT - 1-2 sentences, optional]
Additional background only if essential

[NEXT STEPS & TIMING - 1 sentence]
What happens next and when

[Link to details]
Full analysis/data attached for reference
```

## ✍️ Examples

### Example 1: Decision Request

**Subject**: DECISION: Launch date for Feature X - need by EOW

**Body**:
```
Need your approval to move Feature X launch from March 15
to March 22.

Why the change:
• Critical bug discovered in QA (affects 100% of users)
• Fix requires 5 additional engineering days
• Alternative is launching with known major issue

Impact of delay:
• Affects 20 beta customers (already notified, supportive)
• Delays revenue by 1 week (~$15K)
• No impact to Q1 goals (still within quarter)

Recommend: Approve the delay. Quality over speed aligns
with our brand promise.

Can you confirm by Friday? I'll notify stakeholders Monday.

Full details: [link to project doc]
```

---

### Example 2: FYI Update

**Subject**: FYI: Beta results exceed targets - proceeding to GA

**Body**:
```
Feature X beta exceeded targets. Launching to GA on March 15.

Key results:
• 85% adoption rate (target: 60%)
• NPS: 72 (target: 40)
• 0 critical bugs

Next: Marketing kickoff Monday, GA launch Wed 3/15.

Full results: [link]
```

---

### Example 3: Problem Alert

**Subject**: ALERT: User churn up 5% MoM - mitigation plan ready

**Body**:
```
User churn increased 5% MoM. Root cause identified,
mitigation plan in motion.

What happened:
• Onboarding bug went undetected for 3 weeks
• Affected 1,200 new users (20% of cohort)
• Bug fixed yesterday

Mitigation:
• Re-engagement campaign starting tomorrow
• Personal outreach to affected users (in progress)
• Engineering postmortem completed

Expected recovery: 60% of affected users within 30 days.

I'll update you weekly until resolved. Full analysis: [link]
```

### Example 4: Opportunity

**Subject**: OPPORTUNITY: Early access to 10 enterprise leads - need $25K

**Body**:
```
Opportunity to pilot with 10 enterprise customers (avg deal
size: $150K) if we fund beta program.

The ask: $25K for 3-month pilot program

ROI:
• Potential revenue: $1.5M ARR

• Beta cost: $25K
• Timeline: Close deals in Q3

Risk: If we don't move now, competitors are pitching same
customers next week.

Recommend: Fund the pilot. 24:1 potential ROI.

Need approval by tomorrow to start Monday. Details: [link]
```

## 📊 Presenting Data

### Keep It Simple
- Use clear numbers, not complex analysis
- Show trend, not just point-in-time
- Include "so what" interpretation

**Good**: "Revenue up 15% MoM. Driven by enterprise segment (up 40%). Momentum continues from new sales comp plan."

**Bad**: "Q1 revenue was $1.2M, which represents a delta of $156K versus the prior period of $1.044M, resulting in a growth rate of 14.94% on a month-over-month basis when comparing..."

### Visualizations
- One chart maximum in email body
- Make it dead simple (no interpretation needed)
- More complex analysis goes in attachment

## 🎭 Tone & Style

### Be Confident, Not Arrogant
**Good**: "I recommend Option A because [clear reasoning]"
**Bad**: "Obviously, we should do Option A"

### Be Honest About Problems
**Good**: "We missed the target by 10%. Here's why and how we're fixing it."
**Bad**: "Results were below expectations due to various factors"

### Present Options, Then Recommend
**Good**: "Three options: A, B, C. I recommend B because [reasoning]. Risk: [risk]."
**Bad**: "We could do A or B or C. What do you think?"

### Own the Outcome
**Good**: "I'll own this and report back Friday"
**Bad**: "We'll see what happens and touch base later"

## ⚠️ What to Avoid

### Don't
- ❌ Bury the lede ("I wanted to reach out..." NO!)
- ❌ Provide data without interpretation
- ❌ Present problems without proposed solutions
- ❌ Use qualifiers ("I think maybe we should possibly...")
- ❌ Write long paragraphs (use bullets)
- ❌ Include unnecessary context
- ❌ Ask open-ended questions ("What should we do?")
- ❌ Use jargon or acronyms without definition

### Instead
- ✅ Start with the headline
- ✅ Provide data with "so what"
- ✅ Bring solutions, not just problems
- ✅ Be direct and confident
- ✅ Use bullets and short sentences
- ✅ Include only essential context
- ✅ Come with recommendations
- ✅ Write in plain English

## 🎯 Special Scenarios

### When You Don't Know the Answer
**Don't**: "I'm not sure..."
**Do**: "I don't have that data yet. I'll get it and report back by [time]."

### When You Made a Mistake
**Don't**: Make excuses or hide it
**Do**: "I made a mistake. [What happened]. [How you're fixing it]. [How you'll prevent it]."

### When You Disagree
**Don't**: "I disagree because I think..."
**Do**: "I see it differently. Here's why: [data/reasoning]. Open to your perspective."

### When You Need More Resources
**Don't**: "We need more budget because things cost money"
**Do**: "[Opportunity]. Cost: $X. Expected return: $Y. Timeline: Z. Recommend: Approve."

---

## ✅ Pre-Send Checklist

Before sending to an executive:
- [ ] Does the subject line tell them what I need?
- [ ] Is my ask/headline in the first sentence?
- [ ] Can they understand it in 30 seconds?
- [ ] Have I removed all unnecessary words?
- [ ] Are my key points in bullets?
- [ ] Have I provided a clear recommendation?
- [ ] Is the timeline/deadline clear?
- [ ] Is detailed backup in an attachment, not the email?
- [ ] Have I proofread for typos?

## 📋 Templates

### Decision Request
```
Subject: DECISION: [Topic] - need by [date]

Need your [approval/input] on [decision].

Key factors:
• [Factor 1]
• [Factor 2]
• [Factor 3]

Recommendation: [Your recommendation] because [brief reason].

Timeline: Need decision by [date] because [why].

Details: [link]
```

### FYI Update
```
Subject: FYI: [Headline summary of news]

[One sentence with the news/update]

[Optional: 2-3 bullets with key points]

[Optional: One sentence on next steps]

[Link to details if needed]
```

### Problem + Solution
```
Subject: [ALERT/ISSUE]: [Problem] - [Status]

[One sentence describing problem]

Impact:
• [Impact 1]
• [Impact 2]

Solution:
• [Action 1]
• [Action 2]

Timeline: [When resolved]

Full details: [link]
```
